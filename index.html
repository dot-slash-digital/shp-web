About Susan Hood
Over 27 years of experience, professionalism, and compassion.
Susan believes in the benefits of having an unbiased, supportive voice in the lives of those going through personal, internal struggles. Often times, friends and family lack the proper knowledge to help get through the mental walls many people grapple with. Taking the leap and participating in professional therapy can lead to many benefits, not limited to: improving your mood, reducing your stress or anxiety, resolving long or short-term conflicts, building self-worth, learning healthy coping strategies, and leading a more fulfilling life.
Toting a B.A. in Psychology from UC San Diego, an M.A. in Psychology and a PhD in Clinical Psychology both from Alliant International University (formerly California School of Professional Psychology), Susan Hood has been licensed by the State of Nevada Board of Psychological Examiners since 1996. She specializes in helping individuals work through:
Anxiety
Childhood trauma
Bipolar disorder
Stress
Codependency
Substance abuse
Depression
Relationship issues
PTSD and other conditions

Frequently Asked Questions
Why therapy?
Friends and family don’t always have the knowledge and advice to help one overcome life’s challenges. Therapists can provide a professional opinion and a safe environment to resolve difficult personal issues.
What can therapy help with?
Therapy can help a variety of aspects. Some ways that I’ve helped previous clients include:
  • Improving mood
  • Reducing reactivity/anger
  • Reduction of stress
  • Reduction of anxiety/panic symptoms
  • Improving communication and conflict resolution in relationships
  • Facilitate increased understanding of maladaptive learned behaviors such as:
  • People pleasing
  • Improving self-worth
  • Reduction of feelings of shame
  • Teaching positive/healthy coping strategies versus unhealthy coping strategies (like alcohol, drugs, gambling, etc.)
What are the most common issues that you help people with?
Anxiety-related disorders, stress, childhood trauma and relationships, and mood disorders. Please reach out via the contact form if you have questions on specific issues you would like to address.
How many sessions are required?
There is no required amount of sessions. Susan’s recommended approach is to set therapy goals and continue sessions until those goals are met.
Do you offer in-person therapy sessions?
All sessions are online via Zoom, to provide you with the flexibility and freedom to join therapy from anywhere at any time.
How do I pay for a session?
All costs and fees are collected via Zelle or credit card at the end of each session.
How long should I expect to wait after reaching out to hear back?
Susan will respond promptly to all questions and appointment inquiries, typically within 24 hours.
